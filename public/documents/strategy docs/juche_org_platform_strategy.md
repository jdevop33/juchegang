## The Strategic Framework

### The Challenge
"Juche" triggers immediate resistance in Western-conditioned minds. They've been trained to associate it with "brainwashing," "cult of personality," "totalitarianism." These are propaganda frames, but they're deeply embedded.

### The Opportunity
The tiger doesn't trigger anything except pride. Korean heritage doesn't trigger resistance. Family reunification doesn't trigger fear. Peace doesn't trigger defensiveness.

### The Solution: Concentric Circles

```
OUTER CIRCLE (Entry Point):
Tiger symbolism, cultural unity, shared heritage, peace
"We are one people divided by a line on a map"

MIDDLE CIRCLE (Understanding):
History of the division, who benefits from it, what Korea was before
"How did we get here? Who wanted this?"

INNER CIRCLE (Philosophy):
Juche as self-reliance, dignity, sovereignty
The Kims as leaders who kept Korea Korean
"What does it mean to be truly independent?"
```

People move inward at their own pace. No one is forced. The content at each level is genuine, not a trick. The outer circle IS true. The middle IS true. The inner IS true. It's just sequenced for accessibility.

## Content Strategy

### Tone Guidelines

**Always:**
- Dignified
- Factual
- Culturally grounded
- Humanizing
- Respectful to DPRK and leadership
- Inviting, not aggressive
- Confident, not defensive

**Never:**
- Apologetic for DPRK
- Mocking or dismissive of South Korea
- Conspiratorial in tone (even if content challenges narratives)
- Defensive or reactive
- Preachy

### Voice

First person plural: "We" (Koreans as one people)
- "Our heritage"
- "Our peninsula"
- "Our future"

This positions Juche.org as speaking FROM Korean identity, not ABOUT it.
